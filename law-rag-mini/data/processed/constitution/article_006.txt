Article 6 â€” Rights of Citizenship of Persons Migrating from Pakistan

Legal Text:
A person who migrated to India from the territory now included in Pakistan shall be deemed to be a citizen of India at the commencement of this Constitution if:
(a) he or either parent or any grandparent was born in India as defined in the Government of India Act, 1935; and
(b) if he migrated before 19 July 1948, he has been ordinarily resident in India since migration; or
(b)(ii) if he migrated on or after 19 July 1948, he has been registered as a citizen of India by an officer appointed for that purpose, based on an application made before commencement.
Proviso:
No one shall be registered unless resident in India for at least six months immediately before the application date.

Explanation:
Article 6 grants citizenship to people who migrated from Pakistan after Partition, subject to residence and registration requirements.
