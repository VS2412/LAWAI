Article 15 â€” Prohibition of Discrimination

Legal Text:
(1) The State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.

(2) No citizen shall be subject to any disability or restriction on these grounds regarding:
(a) access to shops, public restaurants, hotels, and public entertainment places,
(b) use of wells, tanks, roads, and public places maintained from State funds or dedicated to the public.

(3) The State may make special provisions for women and children.

(4) The State may make special provisions for socially and educationally backward classes, Scheduled Castes, and Scheduled Tribes.

(5) The State may make special provisions for backward classes, Scheduled Castes, and Scheduled Tribes regarding admission to educational institutions, including private unaided institutions (not minority institutions).

Explanation:
Article 15 forbids discrimination by the State but allows affirmative action for disadvantaged groups, women, and children.
