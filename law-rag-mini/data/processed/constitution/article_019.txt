Article 19 â€” Protection of Certain Rights Regarding Freedom of Speech, etc.

Legal Text:
(1) All citizens shall have the right:
(a) to freedom of speech and expression,
(b) to assemble peaceably and without arms,
(c) to form associations or unions or co-operative societies,
(d) to move freely throughout the territory of India,
(e) to reside and settle in any part of India,
(g) to practise any profession or to carry on any occupation, trade, or business.

(2) Reasonable restrictions on freedom of speech may be imposed in the interests of the sovereignty and integrity of India, security of the State, friendly relations with foreign States, public order, decency, morality, contempt of court, defamation, or incitement to an offence.

(3) Reasonable restrictions may be imposed on the right to assemble in the interests of sovereignty, integrity, or public order.

(4) Reasonable restrictions may be imposed on forming associations in the interests of sovereignty, integrity, public order, or morality.

(5) Reasonable restrictions may be imposed on freedom of movement and residence for the interests of the general public or protection of Scheduled Tribes.

(6) Reasonable restrictions may be imposed on practising any profession or carrying on any business in the interests of the general public. The State may make laws relating to:
(i) professional or technical qualifications, or
(ii) State-controlled industries, trades, or services, even to the exclusion of citizens.

Explanation:
Article 19 lists essential freedoms available only to citizens, and defines specific grounds on which the State may limit these freedoms through reasonable restrictions.
